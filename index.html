Capability Gaps & Hiring Insights

Hiring signals

The fresh posting for a Senior Cybersecurity Engineer – Database on 29 July 2025 suggests GM is bolstering its data security and SIEM capabilities【548506560006845†L60-L104】. However, a single hire may not cover the breadth of responsibilities described (database activity monitoring, DLP, incident response and mentoring). The absence of concurrent postings for incident‑response leads or cloud‑security architects indicates potential skills gaps in those areas.

Regulatory & compliance gaps

The Nebraska AG complaint accuses GM of selling telematics data without consent【312093464664716†L326-L337】. Combined with the Minnesota and California privacy statutes requiring risk assessments and audits【312093464664716†L126-L159】, GM faces increased scrutiny. Recommended Course Sequence

The following SANS courses align with the tensions identified above. Click each course to view a brief description.

SEC504: Hacker Tools, Techniques, Exploits & Incident Handling

Prepares security teams to detect, respond to and recover from security incidents. It covers attacker tools, live forensics and threat containment, making it ideal for addressing data‑breach scenarios like those alleged in the Nebraska lawsuit.

MGT512: Security Leadership Essentials for Managers

Equips managers with the skills to build security programs, translate regulatory requirements into policy and communicate risk to executives. Useful for navigating new state privacy laws and strengthening governance.

SEC510: Public Cloud Security & SEC588: Cloud Penetration Testing

These courses address securing and testing cloud and API environments. As GM expands through joint ventures and telematics services, cloud‑native security skills are critical to protect shared platforms.

FOR578: Cyber Threat Intelligence

Focuses on building and operationalizing threat intelligence. For organizations hiring database and SIEM engineers, this course helps analysts turn logs and threat data into actionable detection rules.

Draft Capability Development Roadmap

This phased plan combines immediate hiring with targeted training to address identified tensions.

Phase Timeline Key Actions
Immediate (0‑3 months) Aug–Oct 2025
  • Onboard the new Senior Cybersecurity Engineer and assign them to develop SIEM & DLP rules.
  • Enroll at least two incident‑response team members in SEC504 to improve breach handling.
  • Conduct a rapid data inventory and privacy gap assessment to respond to the Nebraska lawsuit and state laws.
Mid‑Term (3‑6 months) Nov 2025–Jan 2026
  • Hire or designate a privacy compliance lead to coordinate audits and implement recommendations from MGT512.
  • Send cloud and API engineers to SEC510 or SEC588 for secure design and penetration testing.
  • Create vendor‑risk assessment processes for new joint ventures (e.g., Hyundai collaboration).
Long‑Term (6‑12 months) Feb–Aug 2026
  • Build an internal threat‑intelligence program using skills from FOR578, producing proactive detection rules.
  • Review and update telematics data‑sharing agreements to align with privacy best practices.
  • Institutionalize regular tabletop exercises that simulate breaches across manufacturing plants and telematics services.
